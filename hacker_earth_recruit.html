B2B SaaS Application for Developer Recruitment

Summary

About the company

HackerEarth is a start-up that makes products that help tech companies hire software developers. From its inception in 2012 to present day, they have helped 500+ companies, including Amazon, Intuit, Boeing, Maersk and so on, hire software developers for multiple roles.

About the
product

HackerEarth Recruit, this SaaS application, is one of their main products and is meant for first-level filtering of candidates, i.e., selecting candidates to interview from the entire pool of applicants. On this application, recruiters can create coding tests and invite candidates to take the test. The application's algorithm automatically checks candidates' responses and scores them based on specific parameters set by the recruiter. The application provides detailed reports on the candidates' performance, from which the recruiter can then filter the best.

My role

I joined the company in 2013, when it was an early-stage startup, as their first full-time designer. This product was then in the beta phase and had about 25 enterprise customers.

Problem,
Solution,
Impact

In the beta, the development team was hand-holding the users in the process of creating tests, inviting candidates, and viewing reports. I designed the first public version of this product surfacing the features that the development team was handling on the back-end, thus enabling the users to independently operate the application. It helped the developers focus their energy on building new features and evolving the product to gain more customers rather than on doing tech support for existing customers. After implementation of the changes, this application was marketed heavily and gained 25+ enterprise customers in the next 8 months.

Other team members

Two startup founders, one back-end developer, and one front-end developer

Process

Interviewed company founders and existing users to understand the developer recruitment process

When I joined this project, I was completely unfamiliar with this domain. Since HackerEarth was an early-stage start-up, they did not have any documentation on what they had learned about the industry so far. So, I interviewed the founders to understand how the industry works, who the users are, and what problems the product solves. Additionally, I talked to 7 recruiters from our existing customer-base to understand their recruitment process and pain points.

Understood the rationale for new features and contextualized them using tasks, scenarios, and user roles

When I had joined this project, the start-up founders had given me a list of features they wanted me to include in the new design. I talked to them to understand the rationale for the new features they were proposing. I contextualized these features in the user's lives by mapping the tasks they corresponded to, the scenarios they would be useful in, and the user roles that would use them.

New features contextualized using tasks and scenarios.

Sketched task flows to understand where new features would fit in the interface and created mockups

For each new feature, I sketched user flows based on the tasks and scenarios. They helped me understand where these features would fit in the existing interface. Based on this understanding, I created mockups for the new design.

Feedback after launch

After the public launch, this product was marketed heavily and, as a result, gained 25+ enterprise customers in next 8 months. We got positive feedback from recruiters who conducted tests using this application, along with requests for new features.

I looked at customer support requests and talked to the person responsible for onboarding new customers. I found that the users were able to use the product easily once they had learned how to use it. But onboarding was a challenge because this way of conducting recruitment was new to many companies. I realized that we had not paid attention to the onboarding flow of the application. So I suggested designing UI indicators for onboarding new users. However, that could not be implemented because of other priorities.

My role beyond UX design

Since this was a startup, we never had enough hands to do all the work. I felt invested in the product's success, so it was launched and was being marketed, I helped with writing client communication, creating emailers, designing advertising graphics (both print and digital), designing marketing pages, writing customer support emails, and making marketing presentations.
