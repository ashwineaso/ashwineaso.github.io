About me

I am User Experience designer with 4 years of industry experience in India. I am currently finishing my Master's degree in Human-Computer Interaction at Georgia Institute of Technology and am looking for a full-time role that starts in July. Outside of work, I am a yoga teacher, a seasoned backpacker, and an aspiring vegan chef.

My unique skills


Even though I have handled the end-to-end UX design process in past roles, my strength is in distilling research insights and creating designs that balance user needs, business requirements, and technical constraints. I play the role of the user advocate on my teams by helping contextualize new feature ideas in user scenarios and task flows. I take extra steps to understand the problem before jumping into solving it. I can both rapidly sketch low-fidelity ideas to brainstorm solutions and convert a design vision to a realistic high-fidelity clickable prototype. Having worked with several engineers and written code myself, I am comfortable partnering with engineers and working within technical constraints. I can quickly learn new skills on the job and apply them to projects. I take the initiative to go beyond the brief and am constantly trying to find opportunities to make the product and the process better.

My design journey


I started my career in India as a part-time graphic designer alongside my full-time undergrad degree in Civil Engineering. Then I worked for a year as a UI designer at a design consultancy where I learned about UX. I wanted to be closer to the product and work in collaboration with business and engineering, so I shifted to tech startups. After 2 years of working there as a UX Designer, I moved to the US to study Human-Computer Interaction at Georgia Institute of Technology to get formal education in design.

At Georgia Tech, I added new methods to my toolkit. I learned how to do affinity mapping, storyboarding, quantitative usability testing, and design ideation. I also gained soft skills in articulation, bridging team communication, critical thinking, and rigorous detail orientation.

Outside of work


My passion for design bleeds outside my day job. In 2014, I led the organization of India's first design-themed Startup Weekend, a 3-day startup hack-a-thon that had close to 60 attendees. Our event template and toolkits were also distributed to and appreciated by the global Startup Weekend community. In the same year, I also volunteered to mentor startups in India's Microsoft Accelerator program.

Other than being a designer, I am also a practicing yoga teacher. I have been teaching Hatha yoga since 2015. I have backpacked in more than 10 countries around the world and volunteered to teach kids at a village school in India. These days, I am experimenting with new vegan recipes.
