Redesigning a B2C Saas for Personal Loans

Improved the design of navigation and the text editor in the internal content management application enabling users find and add content easily

The company

ACredy provides small-ticket loans to salaried individuals working at private firms. The entire process is quick, secure and paperless, with a turn around time of 6 hrs.

About the Project

The Credy borrower app is the flagship consumer-facing app of the service. The app streamlines the loan application process to a series of simple steps, with no physical documents involved at any part of the process.

Purpose of the Project

After launching the initial version of the Android app, we collected user feedback through in-app surveys and direct calls. This project encompasses the incremental improvements and enhancements in the design, based on the key insights from the feedback.

My Role in the Project

During the project, I took up the responsibility of designing the process flow, the key feature of the product. My primary responsibility was to design the layouts, along with the wireframes, and prototypes. I also helped the team conduct several experiments around user expectation, through surveys and A/B testing.

Overview

About the Company

Credy is a micro-finance company based out of Bangalore, India; with a very unique vision - The right to credit for everyone.In a country where around 40% of the population live below the international poverty line, and only around 300M can be classified as middle class, there is a dire need for ease and availability of credit.

Credy aims to serve at least a small portion of those people, by evaluating their creditworthiness and providing small-ticket loans. The goal is to make the entire process fast, seamless and paperless.

User Research

We conducted market research in the top 5 cities in India around key factors such as the age group applying for the loan, their employment, annual income, educational qualification, financial knowledge and financial history.

Our primary research was done were done both online — via survey and polls, and offline — user interviews and paper surveys, across multiple industries, backgrounds and standards of living.

For our secondary research, we used financial data and yearly reports published by top consulting companies, along with those published by the Reserve Bank of India — India’s central banking institution.
