Interactive Art Installation

Summary

Class

Social Justice and Design Project Studio

Brief

Choose three words from the poem "Still I Rise" and create a graphical representation. Constraints: Type (Helvetica or Futura), form (square), color (black and white), and medium (paper)

Other team members

Individual project

Design Process

Rapidly sketched 30+ different ideas

I chose three words, 'Hope', 'Slave', 'Dream', and rapidly sketched as many ideas as I could think of.

Digitized the ideas in Illustrator

Evolved the designs based on feedback

Over a period of 3 weeks, I presented several ideas. Many were rejected and some evolved. I realized that, through contrast, I was trying to tell a visual story with the opposing states of black and white but the words I chose didn't reflect any opposition in meaning. So I changed the words to: "Leaving behind nights of terror and fear, I rise".

Created an interactive installation taking inspiration from origami

In the last week of the project, I began thinking of ideas to make this project interactive. I was inspired by an origami form that I used to play with as a child.

This idea was well received by the class, but the scale of this piece was too small to do justice to the strength of the line from the poem. As I was searching for interactive art installations online, I came across this project on Pinterest. I merged these two ideas and created my final piece.

Reflection

I learned through this exercise that the first ideas are not always the best. In the intial stage of brainstorming, it's important to think of multiple, truly different ideas that are not just different versions of the same idea.
